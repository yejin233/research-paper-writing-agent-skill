# Paper Brief

## Thesis

Adaptive temporal weighting improves anomaly detection under gradual distribution
shift without requiring labels at deployment time.

## Contribution

- A label-free weighting mechanism tied to temporal uncertainty.
- A controlled evaluation against static and adaptive baselines.
- An analysis of when the mechanism helps and when rapid drift breaks it.

## Scope

- Task: multivariate time-series anomaly detection.
- Setting: gradual test-time distribution shift.
- Excluded claims: abrupt-shift robustness and causal identification.

## Audience and Venue

- Audience: machine-learning researchers working on reliable time series.
- Target venue: ICLR; verify the current official submission policy.

## Constraints

- Use only public datasets.
- Fit experiments within 200 GPU-hours.
- Do not change the central thesis without author approval.
